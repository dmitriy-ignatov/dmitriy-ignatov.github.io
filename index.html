Problem Statement (Project Management 2)

One‑sentence summary: USC students seeking entry‑level tech‑sales/SE roles lack a clear path to beginner‑friendly, skills‑matched opportunities across fragmented sources.

Full statement: Primary users: USC undergraduates pursuing entry‑level tech‑sales and solutions‑engineering roles who have basic CS coursework and some customer‑facing experience. Problem: These students cannot easily find beginner‑friendly roles that combine technical fundamentals with customer interaction, because openings are scattered across portals and lack clear skill prerequisites. Goal: Provide a curated, skills‑mapped feed of internships, on‑campus projects, and micro‑experiences with checklists, examples, and application tips tailored to tech‑sales/SE pathways. Scope & constraints: Focus on USC and remote roles; require verifiable postings; update weekly; deliver via a simple web page. Success metrics: ≥30 qualified opportunities/month; ≥20% click‑through from students who view the feed; ≥10 student applications/month attributed to the site within 60 days.
